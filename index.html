The whole process happened without neighbors knowing
The county purchased the land in 2021, selected an operator in 2024, approved a 20-year lease, and issued construction permits — all without a single public hearing. Community Development Director Ann Lippmann confirmed this in writing on April 20, 2026. Because the property has been zoned industrial since 1969, no rezoning was needed — and without rezoning, no hearing was ever legally required.
There are no county rules on hours, trucks, or odor
The county applied zero operational conditions during plan review. What time trucks can arrive, where they stage, how odor is managed — none of it is governed by anything Paulding County can enforce. It all falls to the private contract with Capital Waste Services and GA EPD state rules, neither of which neighbors have seen.
No traffic study was done, despite a weight-limit road
The Northwest Georgia Regional Commission exempted this project from a Development of Regional Impact review — which meant no formal traffic study was ever required. Rosedale Drive, the primary truck access road, is a two-lane residential road with a strict 18-ton weight limit. Tractor-trailers will exceed that limit. GDOT is reportedly in ongoing discussions with the county but no plan has been made public.
The county's own attorney flagged a cancer-chemical risk
At the April 14, 2026 Work Session, the county attorney raised PFAS ("forever chemicals") contamination as a real risk tied to the county's waste facilities. The county retained the law firm Arnold and Nitkin LLP on contingency to pursue litigation against PFAS manufacturers — an implicit admission that contamination of groundwater is a foreseeable outcome.
Construction violated a stream buffer and triggered a stop work order
On April 6, 2026, a county inspector issued both a Stop Work Order and a Stream Buffer Violation — meaning work had been done inside the protected buffer zones near the creek. The site was marked "in compliance" the following week, but no corrective action documentation has been made public.
Key documents in the plan set contradict each other
The area of land to be disturbed is reported as 12.24 acres in hydrology and permit documents, and as 10.86 acres in clearing and grading submittals — a 1.38-acre gap. This number determines what erosion controls are required and how much bonding the county holds. Which figure was used for the Land Disturbance Permit is not publicly confirmed.
Neighbors across two counties are already organized and on record
Over 1,660 people signed the Change.org petition. Residents of Menllo Station (Paulding) and Warren Creek (Cobb County) both spoke publicly. Powder Springs Councilmember Raja Antone submitted a formal statement demanding relocation. Cobb County Commissioner Monique Sheffield is actively working with Paulding officials. The Ozora Road transfer station in Gwinnett County was completely withdrawn after similar community opposition — that's a real precedent.
Five concrete actions, in order of priority. Start with Step 1 — nothing else is enforceable until you know what rules actually apply.
1
Get the two documents that govern everything
All operational rules — hours, truck staging, odor, noise — live in exactly two documents: the Capital Waste Services operating contract and the GA EPD Notification of Permit by Rule (Section 391-3-4-.06(3)(b)). File an Open Records Request under the Georgia Open Records Act (O.C.G.A. § 50-18-70). The county must respond within 3 business days.
Also request: Phase 3 Final Erosion Control Plan (and its submission date vs. the Dec 22, 2025 LDP) · April 6, 2026 Stop Work Order documentation and any GA EPD corrective action · The official LOD figure used for the LDP (12.24 or 10.86 acres) · The NW Georgia Regional Commission DRI exemption letter
File with: Paulding County Clerk's Office

4
Use the county's own PFAS admission as leverage
The county attorney's April 14, 2026 statement is a gift: by retaining Arnold and Nitkin LLP to sue PFAS manufacturers, the county implicitly acknowledged contamination of county waste facilities is a real, foreseeable risk. Push on this at every public meeting.
Ask the BOC: what baseline water testing will be done on wells and waterways near Reynolds Road before operations begin? · Request the same PFAS monitoring standards the county is seeking from manufacturers be applied to this facility · Monitor whether the litigation scope covers Reynolds Road or only existing landfills
5
Organize around specific, winnable demands
The cross-county coalition — 1,660+ petition signers, Menllo Station, Warren Creek, Councilmember Antone, Commissioner Sheffield — represents real political pressure. The Ozora Road transfer station in Gwinnett County was withdrawn after sustained community opposition. Focus on demands that are concrete and enforceable:
· County-level operating conditions written into a public agreement (no trucks before 7 AM, odor monitoring, noise limits)
· Expanded wooded buffer zones beyond the 75-ft minimum
· Mandatory PFAS baseline water testing before operations begin
· A formal traffic plan for Rosedale Drive with GDOT sign-off
· Annual public meeting with Capital Waste Services and county staff

Process & notice
Why were no public hearings held at any stage — not for the land purchase, the operator selection, or the permitting?
Why was the property purchase placed on the consent agenda rather than as a standalone item with public comment?
When and how were neighboring residents first notified that this facility was being planned?
Traffic & operations
Why did the Regional Commission exempt this project from a DRI review — and therefore from a traffic study — despite documented concerns about Rosedale Drive?
Does Rosedale Drive's 18-ton weight limit apply to transfer station trucks? Who enforces it and who pays for road damage?
The county imposed zero operational conditions. What recourse do neighbors have if trucks arrive at 3:00 AM?
Documents & environment
The Limits of Disturbance are listed as 12.24 acres in some documents and 10.86 acres in others. Which figure was used for the LDP?
Was the Phase 3 Erosion Control Plan finalized before the LDP was issued in December 2025?
Given the county attorney's PFAS admission, what baseline water testing will be done near Reynolds Road before operations begin?
What resulted from the April 6, 2026 stream buffer violation? Was a corrective action report filed with GA EPD?
The 20-year lease
What are the county's exit terms if community or environmental concerns escalate?
